\begin{abstract}
\noindent A fixed pessimistic belief has little price relevance for a safe sovereign or for one already near certain default. It matters at the boundary between them. I place this force in a sovereign bond model with narrative diffusion and debt-service feedback. Investors first become aware of a claim and then accept it after a noisy verification signal. The state therefore satisfies $0\leq n_t\leq a_t\leq1$, where $a_t$ is awareness and $n_t$ is belief. Exact one-year risk-neutral pricing maps default probability into the bond yield. The primitive belief gap is single-peaked, and a derivative condition preserves this hump in equilibrium. Numerical continuation finds \EquilibriumCount\ steady states, classifies their eigenvalues, and locates the belief seed that separates survival from default. At the boundary experiment, the threshold is \SeedThresholdPct\ percent and the baseline two percent seed crosses it. Ten percentage points of belief raise the spread by at most \RelPeakTen\ basis points. The two-year default wedge peaks at \WedgePeak\ percentage points. A rank-two measurement system uses media, search, analyst, and survey indicators to identify awareness and belief separately.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

On July 26, 2012, the president of the European Central Bank stated that the ECB was ready to do whatever was necessary within its mandate to preserve the euro. The ECB announced the Outright Monetary Transactions program in September and never activated it. Italian and Spanish short yields fell sharply around these announcements \citep{AltavillaEtAl2016}. OMT changed redenomination insurance, conditional policy support, and the expected regime \citep{KrishnamurthyEtAl2018}. It provides a descriptive case of how a backstop can change both prices and crisis narratives.

This paper asks when a pessimistic claim can move a sovereign spread. The answer comes from the default boundary. A fixed belief distortion changes few decisions when default is remote. It also changes little when default is already nearly certain. Between these tails lies a narrow region in which beliefs move risk-neutral default compensation, borrowing costs, and future repayment capacity.

The direct belief gap is $G(z)=\Phi(z+\Delta)-\Phi(z)$. It is the probability mass between the ordinary and pessimistic default thresholds. This gap has one peak at $z=-\Delta/2$ and vanishes in both tails. The equilibrium effect multiplies $G$ by the spread-feedback term. Proposition~\ref{prop:eqhump} gives the derivative condition under which feedback preserves that peak, and the quantitative scenario verifies it on the full state space.

The contagion block separates attention from acceptance. Encounter with a believer can make an investor aware. The investor then receives a noisy verification signal and accepts the claim only when its posterior odds cross zero. Awareness and belief follow distinct continuous-time laws. This separation permits media and search measures to load mainly on awareness while investor surveys and analyst positions load on belief.

When $R_0<1$, a small belief seed decays. When $R_0>1$, it grows while fundamentals remain in the susceptible interval. Debt-service feedback then changes the fundamentals path. The full state is $(\theta,a,n)$. Numerical continuation finds a lower no-story root at \LowerRoot, a positive-belief saddle at $\theta=\SaddleTheta$, and a stable safe state at \StableTheta. Along the initial-awareness curve used in the experiment, a \SeedThresholdPct\ percent belief seed separates survival from default. The baseline seed crosses capacity zero in month \ZoneDefMonth, while the unseeded economy converges to the safe state.

The model moves prices more than default frequencies. At the hump's peak, ten percentage points of belief raise the spread by \RelPeakTen\ basis points. Along the seeded boundary path, the excess spread reaches \PeakExcess\ basis points. The same mechanism raises the two-year default probability by at most \WedgePeak\ percentage points at baseline feedback and by \WedgeChiLo\ to \WedgeChiHi\ points across the stable financing range. For a safe sovereign the addition is \WedgeSafe\ points.

Policy can reduce encounters, raise belief abandonment, or cap the modelled bond yield. A one-time intervention delays a supercritical outbreak. Faster abandonment lowers the reproduction number. A binding cap removes the marginal narrative effect from the capped bond and truncates its debt-service feedback.

\citet{FlynnSastry2024} build a theory, measurement system, and quantitative model of contagious firm narratives in a unique-equilibrium business cycle. They measure narratives in earnings calls and regulatory filings, then connect belief diffusion to firm decisions and aggregate fluctuations. This paper places contagious beliefs in sovereign debt. The default boundary creates a hump restriction on price relevance. Price relevance feeds back into diffusion incentives, and borrowing costs move the real default state. The empirical test therefore interacts filtered investor belief with predetermined boundary proximity. Text methods supply the observable media process \citep{GentzkowKellyTaddy2019}.

The empirical design combines GDELT with licensed full text, search intensity, analyst reports, and survey beliefs. Scheduled fiscal releases discipline timing. Cross-fitting and empirical Bayes shrinkage estimate boundary proximity before the narrative window. A joint bootstrap carries first-stage uncertainty into the hump regression. OMT remains a descriptive compound event.

\section{A Sovereign, Its Creditors, and a Story}\label{sec:model}

The threshold-default primitive delivers the direct pricing hump. Equilibrium single-peakedness adds a derivative restriction on the pricing feedback. Attention responds to decision value, while belief acceptance follows verification. The global analysis solves the full state system and continues its seed boundary.

\subsection{Fundamentals, default, and pricing}

Time is monthly and the pricing horizon is one year. A sovereign's fundamentals $\theta_t$ measure debt-service capacity in units of annual resources. Fundamentals mean-revert and are drained by borrowing costs:
\begin{equation}
\theta_{t+1} = \rho_m \theta_t + (1 - \rho_m)\bar\theta - \chi_m s_t + \varepsilon_{t+1},
\qquad \varepsilon \sim N(0, \sigma_m^2),
\label{eq:theta-law}
\end{equation}
where $s_t$ is an annualized spread. Default is absorbing once $\theta_t<0$. The monthly coefficients aggregate exactly to the annual law under a constant spread:
\begin{equation}
\rho_m=\rho^{1/12},
\qquad
\sigma_m=\sigma\sqrt{\frac{1-\rho_m^2}{1-\rho^2}},
\qquad
\chi_m=\chi\frac{1-\rho_m}{1-\rho}.
\label{eq:aggregation}
\end{equation}
Thus twelve monthly innovations have variance $\sigma^2$, and twelve monthly spread charges cumulate to $\chi s$ after persistence is accounted for. At the annual horizon, the perceived default probability is
\begin{equation}
p(\theta, s) = \Phi\!\left( z(\theta, s) \right),
\qquad
z(\theta, s) = \frac{-\rho\theta - (1-\rho)\bar\theta + \chi s}{\sigma}.
\label{eq:pdef}
\end{equation}
The S-shape of $p$ in $\theta$ is the paper's load-bearing object, and it comes free: any default technology in which a shock-buffeted state crosses a threshold produces one.

A pessimistic narrative circulates among the sovereign's creditors. A believer prices the bond under fundamentals lower by $\xi$. With believing wealth share $n_t$, the market-clearing risk-neutral default assessment is
\begin{equation}
\bar p^Q(\theta,s,n)=(1-n)\Phi(z)+n\Phi(z+\Delta),
\qquad \Delta \equiv \frac{\rho \xi}{\sigma},
\label{eq:pbar}
\end{equation}
so belief shifts the standardized distance to default by $\Delta$. A one-year zero-coupon bond with risk-free rate $r$, recovery $\mathcal R$, and risk-neutral default probability $p^Q$ has price
\[
q=e^{-r}\left[1-(1-\mathcal R)p^Q\right].
\]
Its default-compensation spread is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal C(p^Q)=-\log\left[1-(1-\mathcal R)p^Q\right].
\label{eq:hazard-price}
\end{equation}
If $p^Q=1-e^{-\lambda^Q}$, then $\mathcal C(p^Q)=(1-\mathcal R)\lambda^Q+O((\lambda^Q)^2)$. The quoted spread adds liquidity premium $LP$ and risk premium $RP$. Narrative belief moves $\mathcal C(\bar p^Q)$, while $LP$ and $RP$ remain fixed in the baseline. The physical simulation sets the market price of aggregate shock risk to zero, so $p^P=p^Q$, and sensitivity can estimate a separate risk-premium process. The within-period equilibrium solves
\begin{equation}
s=\mathcal C\!\left(\bar p^Q(\theta,s,n)\right)+LP+RP.
\label{eq:spread-fixed-point}
\end{equation}

\begin{lemma}[Pricing equilibrium and the doom-loop multiplier]\label{lem:pricing}
Suppose $0<\mathcal R\leq1$ and let $d(z,n)=(1-n)\phi(z)+n\phi(z+\Delta)$. If
\begin{equation}
\sup_{z\in\mathbb R,\,n\in[0,1]}
\mathcal C'\!\left((1-n)\Phi(z)+n\Phi(z+\Delta)\right)
\frac{\chi}{\sigma}d(z,n)<1,
\label{eq:pricing-contraction}
\end{equation}
equation~\eqref{eq:spread-fixed-point} has a unique solution for every $(\theta,n)$. The spread rises with belief and falls with fundamentals. Small probability changes receive the multiplier
\[
M(\theta,n)=\left[1-\mathcal C'(\bar p^Q)
\frac{\chi}{\sigma}d(z^*,n)\right]^{-1}\geq1,
\]
which reaches \MultPeak\ at the reference maximum. The maximal fixed-point gain is \GainMax.
\end{lemma}

The scenario sits inside the uniqueness region with maximal loop gain \GainMax.

\subsection{The contagion block}

Let $a_t$ denote the share aware of the narrative and let $n_t$ denote the share that believes it. Believers form a subset of aware investors. An encounter with a believer generates attention at rate
\begin{equation}
\kappa(\theta,n)=\bar m A H_c\!\left(\lambda\left|\frac{\partial s^*}{\partial n}\right|\right).
\label{eq:attention-rate}
\end{equation}
After becoming attentive, investor $i$ observes a verification signal $r_i=\ell_i+u_i$, where $\ell_i$ is the prior log odds in favor of the claim and $u_i$ is noisy evidence. The investor accepts the claim when $r_i\geq0$. Let $q=\Pr(r_i\geq0)$. A false claim can therefore attract believers when priors or verification noise place positive mass above zero. Awareness and belief follow
\begin{equation}
\dot a_t=\kappa(\theta_t,n_t)n_t(1-a_t)-\gamma_a a_t,
\qquad
\dot n_t=q\kappa(\theta_t,n_t)n_t(1-a_t)-\gamma_n n_t.
\label{eq:sir}
\end{equation}
The reference calculation sets $q=\AcceptVal$, $\gamma_a=\GamAVal$, and $\gamma_n=\GamNVal$ per month. The simulation advances \eqref{eq:sir} with fourth-order Runge--Kutta integration and verifies convergence across 8, 16, and 32 substeps.

\begin{proposition}[Invariant cognitive state]\label{prop:invariant}
Suppose $0\leq q\leq1$, $\kappa\geq0$, and $\gamma_n\geq\gamma_a\geq0$. The set
\[
\mathcal K=\{(a,n):0\leq n\leq a\leq1\}
\]
is forward invariant under \eqref{eq:sir}. At zero belief, no belief appears without a seed. At full awareness, awareness falls. Along $a=n$, the vector field points into $n\leq a$.
\end{proposition}

At zero awareness and belief, the linearized belief equation grows at rate $q\kappa(\theta,0)-\gamma_n$. For $\gamma_n>0$, the reproduction number is
\begin{equation}
R_0(\theta)=\frac{q\bar m A H_c\!\left(\lambda v(\theta,0)\right)}{\gamma_n},
\qquad v(\theta,n)=\frac{\partial s^*(\theta,n)}{\partial n}.
\label{eq:r0}
\end{equation}
Price relevance governs attention. Verification beliefs govern acceptance. Their product determines whether a small belief seed grows.

\section{The Boundary Theorems}\label{sec:boundary}

\subsection{Where stories are priced}

\begin{theorem}[Primitive pricing hump]\label{prop:hump}
Let $G(z)=\Phi(z+\Delta)-\Phi(z)$ for $\Delta>0$. The function $G$ is strictly increasing on $(-\infty,-\Delta/2)$, strictly decreasing on $(-\Delta/2,\infty)$, and converges to zero in both tails. Its unique maximum is $2\Phi(\Delta/2)-1$. Because $z^*(\theta,n)$ is strictly decreasing in $\theta$, the direct belief gap $G(z^*)$ is single-peaked in fundamentals.
\end{theorem}

The equilibrium price impact is
\[
\frac{\partial s^*}{\partial n}
=\mathcal C'(\bar p^Q)\big[\Phi(z^*+\Delta)-\Phi(z^*)\big]M(\theta,n).
\]
\begin{proposition}[Sufficient condition for an equilibrium hump]\label{prop:eqhump}
Put $P(z,n)=(1-n)\Phi(z)+n\Phi(z+\Delta)$ and $d(z,n)=P_z(z,n)$. Define
\[
Q(z,n)=\frac{\partial}{\partial z}
\log\left[\frac{\mathcal C'(P(z,n))G(z)}
{1-\mathcal C'(P(z,n))(\chi/\sigma)d(z,n)}\right].
\]
On an index interval $\mathcal Z$, suppose the pricing gain lies below one and $Q_z(z,n)<0$. The equilibrium price impact has at most one interior peak on the corresponding fundamentals interval. It has exactly one peak if $Q$ takes both signs. In the reference calculation, the largest value of $Q_z$ on $z\in[-6,6]$ and $n\in[0,1]$ is \HumpMargin. The equilibrium effect is therefore single-peaked on the computed state space.
\end{proposition}

The theorem concerns the direct belief gap. The proposition states the additional restriction required for equilibrium feedback to preserve one peak. Boundedness of the multiplier alone is not sufficient.

\subsection{Where stories spread}

Attention rises with equilibrium price impact through equation~\eqref{eq:attention-rate}. The interval result therefore uses Proposition~\ref{prop:eqhump}.

\begin{proposition}[The susceptible zone]\label{prop:zone}
Suppose $\gamma_n>0$, Proposition~\ref{prop:eqhump} holds, and $H_c$ is strictly increasing over the attained values. Then $R_0(\theta)$ is single-peaked with vanishing tails. The susceptible set $Z=\{\theta:R_0(\theta)\geq1\}$ is an interval. It is nonempty if and only if $q\bar m A H_c(\lambda v_{\max})\geq\gamma_n$. A rise in $A$ expands every superlevel set. A rise in $\gamma_n$ contracts it. Changes in verification quality act through $q$.
\end{proposition}

Reducing amplification to one quarter of baseline shrinks the susceptible interval to width \ZoneWidthPre\ in capacity units. At baseline the interval maps into spreads between \ZoneSpreadLo\ and \ZoneSpreadHi\ basis points.

\begin{corollary}[Boundary proxy]\label{cor:stat}
For small $\Delta$, the direct belief gap satisfies $G(z)=\Delta\phi(z)+O(\Delta^2)$. The lagged local sensitivity of spreads to scheduled fundamental surprises therefore supplies a proxy for boundary proximity. Recovery, risk premia, and the feedback multiplier enter the first-stage measurement model separately.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{From local drift to global paths}

The state $(\theta_t,a_t,n_t)$ moves through a three-dimensional region. At zero belief, a small seed decays where $R_0<1$ and grows where $R_0>1$. Global behavior depends on the nullclines and their stability.

\begin{proposition}[Instantaneous drift boundary]\label{prop:cliff}
Let
\[
D(\theta,n)=(1-\rho_m)(\bar\theta-\theta)-\chi_m s^*(\theta,n).
\]
Suppose that for each $n$ in an interval $N$, the equation $D(\theta,n)=0$ has a lower root $\hat\theta(n)>0$ with $D_\theta(\hat\theta(n),n)>0$. Then $\hat\theta$ is continuously differentiable and
\[
\hat\theta'(n)=\frac{\chi_m s_n^*(\hat\theta(n),n)}{D_\theta(\hat\theta(n),n)}>0.
\]
For a fixed belief share, this root is the instantaneous lower drift boundary for the fundamentals equation.
\end{proposition}

The numerical continuation solves the complete three-dimensional steady-state system. It finds \EquilibriumCount\ intersections. The lower no-story state has two positive eigenvalues. The positive-belief state has awareness \SaddleAwarePct\ percent, belief \SaddleBeliefPct\ percent, and largest eigenvalue \SaddleEigen. It is a saddle. The safe no-story state has largest eigenvalue \StableEigen\ and is locally stable. The cognitive state set in Proposition~\ref{prop:invariant} is forward invariant, and default is absorbing.

To trace the basin boundary, the algorithm fixes initial awareness at four times initial belief and bisects on the belief seed for each $\theta_0\in[0.01,0.15]$. At $\theta_0=0.045$, the 240-month threshold is \SeedThresholdPct\ percent. Extending the classification horizon to 360 months changes it by \SeedHorizonDiff\ percentage points. Moving from 16 to 32 Runge--Kutta substeps changes it by \SeedStepDiff\ points. The baseline two percent seed lies above the boundary and defaults in month \ZoneDefMonth. The matched unseeded path converges to the safe state.

\section{Policy Experiments}\label{sec:policy}

\begin{proposition}[Policy effects within the model]\label{prop:policy}
Suppose $\gamma_n>0$. If $R_0>1$ is constant while belief remains near zero, removal of fraction $f$ delays return to the original belief share by
\[
\frac{\log[1/(1-f)]}{\gamma_n(R_0-1)}.
\]
A rise in $\gamma_n$ lowers $R_0$ pointwise. A perfectly credible cap on the modelled bond sets $\partial s^*/\partial n=0$ wherever it binds. When $H_c(0)=0$, new awareness through this price-relevance channel stops. Awareness and belief then decay at rates $\gamma_a$ and $\gamma_n$. The cap also truncates the feedback from that bond spread to fundamentals.
\end{proposition}

The model cap isolates the sovereign bond pricing channel. CDS, other maturities, auction quantities, capital flight, bank funding, and political choices provide additional channels. A policy backstop also changes expected recovery and the policy regime. The calculation below holds those margins fixed, while the OMT event study measures their joint movement.

If attention tracks decision value, a binding cap lowers media diffusion through this channel. A perceived-mispricing mechanism instead permits coverage to persist while the capped yield remains stable. Post-OMT text describes which pattern occurred, while the event study treats OMT as a joint change in tail insurance and the policy regime.

\section{Measurement and Empirical Designs}\label{sec:evidence}

\subsection{Text and the latent narrative state}

The public core corpus is GDELT 2.0 Global Knowledge Graph and Mentions data, downloadable through BigQuery \citep{GDELTData}. Factiva or LexisNexis provides licensed full-text validation. Google Trends measures search attention \citep{GoogleTrendsData}. Consensus forecasts and analyst commentary measure professional beliefs. Survey probability questions supply a direct belief indicator.

A narrative is a structured claim. It names an economic state, gives a causal explanation, implies a future sovereign outcome, and carries an action or policy implication. A high-recall dictionary screen selects candidates. A multilingual classifier assigns the structured label. Two coders label the validation sample under blinded assignment. The research design freezes the prompt, model version, label rules, and country-language error matrices before outcome analysis.

Let $Z^r_{it}$ denote indicator $r$. The measurement system is
\begin{equation}
Z^r_{it}=c^r_i+\lambda^r_a a_{it}+\lambda^r_n n_{it}+e^r_{it},
\qquad r=1,\ldots,R,
\label{eq:measurement7}
\end{equation}
together with the transition law \eqref{eq:sir}. The loading matrix $\Lambda=[\lambda_a,\lambda_n]$ must have rank two. One media loading fixes the awareness scale, and a survey probability loading fixes the belief scale. GDELT, licensed press, and search intensity load primarily on awareness. Analyst direction and survey probabilities load on belief. The state-space likelihood estimates cross-loadings and indicator-specific error. Rank and normalization restrictions assign the percentage-point scale to filtered belief.

\subsection{Boundary interaction and release timing}

The workhorse local projection is
\begin{equation}
\Delta s_{i,t+h}=\alpha_i+\tau_t+f_h(\widetilde B_{i,t-1})\widehat n_{it}
+\Gamma_h'X_{i,t-1}+u_{i,t+h},
\label{eq:lp7}
\end{equation}
where the state-space model supplies filtered belief $\widehat n_{it}$ and bins or splines estimate $f_h$. The theory predicts a small effect in both tails and an interior peak.

The raw boundary proxy is the lagged local sensitivity of spreads to scheduled fundamental surprises. I estimate it on a disjoint training fold, shrink the country coefficient toward a fundamentals-index prediction with empirical Bayes, and carry the posterior mean $\widetilde B_{i,t-1}$ into the outcome fold. The fundamentals index uses debt service, reserves, maturity structure, and primary-balance forecasts. The procedure rotates folds across years and countries. A clustered bootstrap resamples release episodes, re-estimates the boundary coefficient and shrinkage rule, refilters $(a,n)$, and reruns \eqref{eq:lp7}. This joint bootstrap captures first-stage attenuation and the sampling uncertainty in the location of the hump.

For scheduled fiscal release $e$, estimate
\begin{align}
\mathrm{Takeup}_{ie}&=a_i+\tau_e+\pi_1\mathrm{Surprise}_{ie}
+\pi_2\mathrm{Surprise}_{ie}\widetilde B_{i,e-1}+\varepsilon_{ie},

+b_3\widehat\varepsilon_{ie}\widetilde B_{i,e-1}+u_{ie}.
\label{eq:release7}
\end{align}
Eurostat publishes dated calendars for government deficit and debt releases \citep{EurostatCalendar}. These equations measure conditional timing around a known information event. Political commentary, policy responses, and market feedback can also move residual take-up, so the causal test remains equation~\eqref{eq:lp7} with predetermined and cross-fitted states.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

The default boundary gives a precise restriction on narrative pricing. A fixed pessimistic shift changes risk-neutral default compensation by the probability mass between two default thresholds. This mass has one peak. Exact bond pricing and debt-service feedback preserve that peak under the derivative condition in Proposition~\ref{prop:eqhump}.

Awareness and belief form separate states. Price relevance governs attention, while verification signals govern acceptance. Their joint dynamics remain inside $0\leq n\leq a\leq1$. The three-dimensional system has a stable safe state and a positive-belief saddle. Numerical continuation places the belief seed threshold at \SeedThresholdPct\ percent in the boundary experiment.

At the reference inputs, ten percentage points of belief raise the spread by as much as \RelPeakTen\ basis points, and seeding raises two-year default probability by \WedgePeak\ percentage points. The empirical design identifies awareness and belief from a rank-two indicator system. Cross-fitting and shrinkage estimate default-boundary proximity, while a joint bootstrap carries generated-regressor error into the hump. This combination turns a general contagion mechanism into a sovereign-specific restriction on when beliefs move prices and repayment capacity.

\section{Proofs}\label{app:proofs}

\subsection{Proof of Lemma \ref{lem:pricing}}
Write $F(s)=\mathcal C(\bar p^Q(\theta,s,n))+LP+RP$. Since
\[
\mathcal C'(p)=\frac{1-\mathcal R}{1-(1-\mathcal R)p},
\]
the fixed-point derivative is
\[
F'(s)=\mathcal C'(\bar p^Q)\frac{\chi}{\sigma}d(z,n).
\]
Condition~\eqref{eq:pricing-contraction} makes $F$ a contraction on $[LP+RP,-\log\mathcal R+LP+RP]$. Banach's theorem gives a unique fixed point. Implicit differentiation gives
\[
s_n^*=\mathcal C'(\bar p^Q)G(z^*)M(\theta,n)>0,
\qquad
s_\theta^*=-\mathcal C'(\bar p^Q)d(z^*,n)\frac{\rho}{\sigma}M(\theta,n)<0.
\]
A cap replaces $F$ by $\min\{F,\bar s\}$ and preserves the contraction modulus. $\blacksquare$

\subsection{Proof of Proposition \ref{prop:invariant}}
At $n=0$, equation~\eqref{eq:sir} gives $\dot n=0$. At $a=1$, $\dot a=-\gamma_a\leq0$. Along the boundary $a=n$,
\[
\dot a-\dot n=(1-q)\kappa n(1-n)+(\gamma_n-\gamma_a)n\geq0.
\]
At $a=0$, feasibility implies $n=0$. The vector field therefore points inward on every face of $\mathcal K$. Standard invariance for a locally Lipschitz vector field gives forward invariance. $\blacksquare$

\subsection{Proof of Theorem \ref{prop:hump}}
Let $G(z)=\Phi(z+\Delta)-\Phi(z)=\int_z^{z+\Delta}\phi(u)\,du$. Since $\phi'(u)=-u\phi(u)$,
\[
G'(z)=\phi(z+\Delta)-\phi(z).
\]
For $\Delta>0$, equality $\phi(z+\Delta)=\phi(z)$ holds iff $(z+\Delta)^2=z^2$, hence iff $z=-\Delta/2$. If $z<-\Delta/2$, then $|z+\Delta|<|z|$ and $G'(z)>0$. If $z>-\Delta/2$, then $|z+\Delta|>|z|$ and $G'(z)<0$. Thus $G$ is single-peaked at $-\Delta/2$ and converges to zero in both tails. Differentiating the pricing fixed point gives
\[
\frac{dz^*}{d\theta}=-\frac{\rho}{\sigma}\,M(\theta,n)<0,
\]
so the single-peaked profile in $z$ maps into a single-peaked profile in fundamentals. At $z=-\Delta/2$, symmetry gives $G=2\Phi(\Delta/2)-1$. $\blacksquare$

\subsection{Proof of Proposition \ref{prop:eqhump}}
For fixed $n$, write the equilibrium price derivative as
\[
v(z,n)=\frac{c(z,n)G(z)}{1-c(z,n)k d(z,n)},
\qquad c(z,n)=\mathcal C'(P(z,n)),\quad k=\chi/\sigma.
\]
The contraction condition makes the denominator positive. Logarithmic differentiation gives
\[
\frac{\partial\log v(z,n)}{\partial z}=Q(z,n).
\]
If $Q_z<0$, the log derivative is strictly decreasing and has at most one zero. If it takes a positive and a negative value, that zero exists and is the unique maximum. Since $z^*(\theta,n)$ is strictly decreasing in $\theta$, the same uniqueness holds in fundamentals. For computation, use $c_z=c^2d$ and define $B=c^2d^2+cd'$. Direct differentiation gives
\[
Q_z=B+\frac{G''G-(G')^2}{G^2}
+k\frac{B'(1-ckd)+kB^2}{(1-ckd)^2},
\qquad
B'=2c^3d^3+3c^2dd'+cd''.
\]
The grid evaluates this expression directly. $\blacksquare$

\subsection{Proof of Proposition \ref{prop:zone}}
Proposition~\ref{prop:eqhump} makes $v(\theta,0)$ single-peaked with zero tails. Strict monotonicity of $H_c$ preserves that ordering. Multiplication by $q\bar mA/\gamma_n$ preserves it again. A continuous single-peaked function has interval superlevel sets. The set at level one is nonempty exactly when its maximum weakly exceeds one. Increasing $A$ raises the function pointwise and increasing $\gamma_n$ lowers it pointwise, which gives the set inclusions. $\blacksquare$

\subsection{Proof of Proposition \ref{prop:cliff}}
The implicit function theorem applies at the lower root because $D_\theta>0$. Lemma~\ref{lem:pricing} gives $s_n^*>0$. Hence $D_n=-\chi_m s_n^*<0$ and
\[
\hat\theta'(n)=-\frac{D_n}{D_\theta}
=\frac{\chi_m s_n^*}{D_\theta}>0.
\]
The numerical continuation in Section~\ref{sec:boundary} supplies the corresponding global basin classification for the calibrated vector field. $\blacksquare$

\subsection{Proof of Proposition \ref{prop:policy}}
Near zero belief with constant rates, $n(t)=n(0)\exp[\gamma_n(R_0-1)t]+o(n(0))$. Comparing the original and reduced seeds gives the delay formula. Raising $\gamma_n$ lowers $R_0=q\kappa/\gamma_n$. Where a cap binds, the modelled spread is locally constant in $n$. If $H_c(0)=0$, then $\kappa=0$ and equation~\eqref{eq:sir} reduces to $\dot a=-\gamma_a a$ and $\dot n=-\gamma_n n$. The fundamentals drift uses the capped spread. $\blacksquare$

\section{The Pricing Block: Details and the Multiplicity Frontier}\label{app:pricing}

\paragraph{Spread decomposition.} Equation~\eqref{eq:hazard-price} prices one-year default compensation exactly. Writing $p^Q=1-e^{-\lambda^Q}$ gives
\[
\mathcal C(p^Q)=-\log\left[\mathcal R+(1-\mathcal R)e^{-\lambda^Q}\right].
\]
Its first-order term is $(1-\mathcal R)\lambda^Q$. Liquidity and risk premia enter additively in \eqref{eq:spread-fixed-point}. The reference calculation sets them to zero and attributes narrative effects only to $p^Q$. An empirical implementation estimates both components from maturity-matched bonds and CDS.

\paragraph{The Calvo frontier.} Condition~\eqref{eq:pricing-contraction} is sufficient for global contraction. The reference maximal gain is \GainMax. It reaches \GainChiHi\ at $\chi=\ChiGFNHi$ and \GainChiBoundary\ at $\chi=\ChiBoundary$. The latter crosses one and requires pricing-equilibrium selection \citep{Calvo1988,AguiarEtAl2022}.

\paragraph{Wealth-weighted pricing.} Equation~\eqref{eq:pbar} defines $n$ as a wealth share. The measurement system combines quantity-weighted analyst and survey indicators with media awareness. Portfolio rebalancing can make believing wealth differ from believing headcount, which the belief loading absorbs.

\section{Extensions}\label{app:ext}

\paragraph{Cross-border story networks.} Let awareness and belief in country $i$ respond to separate reach-weighted sums of foreign awareness and belief. The two-network system generates correlated spread movements among susceptible countries and supplies the model counterpart to the foreign-diffusion design \citep{LongstaffEtAl2011}.

\paragraph{Endogenous amplification.} Attention to sovereign claims rises with volatility, making $A$ procyclical. The susceptible zone then widens as fundamentals deteriorate. This force has the same empirical signature as a rising pricing multiplier.

\paragraph{Immunity and waves.} Investors who rejected a claim once may resist it later. An SIRS structure with slow waning of immunity produces refractory periods and recurring waves. Conditional on boundary proximity, a repeated claim should spread more slowly on its second arrival.

\paragraph{Heterogeneous claims.} A mixture over bite sizes $\xi$ concentrates the priced composition toward severe claims as the boundary nears because the hump's window widens with $\Delta$. The classification layer can test whether the severity composition rises with measured boundary proximity.
