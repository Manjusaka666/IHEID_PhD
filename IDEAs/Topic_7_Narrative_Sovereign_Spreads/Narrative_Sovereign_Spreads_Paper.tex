\begin{abstract}
\noindent Stories about sovereign default spread from investor to investor, and sometimes they move spreads. I put a contagious pessimistic narrative into a sovereign debt-pricing model with a default boundary and a feedback from borrowing costs to fundamentals. The pricing side is a theorem from one primitive: because default probability is an S-curve in fundamentals, a story that shifts perceived fundamentals is priced in proportion to the curve's local slope. The contagion side rests on one stated behavioral assumption, that stories spread when they are worth acting on, under which the contagion rate inherits the same hump. The state space splits into three derived regions: an immune region where the story dies regardless of seeding, a febrile zone where it goes viral and is priced but self-limits, and a critical zone where the narrative-spread-fundamentals loop moves the default boundary toward the sovereign. In a calibration anchored to sovereign haircut and spread magnitudes, a story at ten percent prevalence is worth \RelPeakTen\ basis points at the boundary and \RelSafeTen\ off it, and a seeded outbreak adds up to \WedgePeak\ percentage points to the two-year default hazard at the baseline feedback, \WedgeChiLo\ to \WedgeChiHi\ across the gross-financing-need band, all inside the zone. Policy divides sharply: deleting three quarters of the story's carriers buys about six weeks, transparency dampens, and a credible spread cap sterilizes the epidemic itself, at \DelayCost\ percentage points of default probability per year of delay.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

On July 26, 2012, the president of the European Central Bank told a London investment conference that the ECB was ready to do \enquote{whatever it takes to preserve the euro.} No facility existed. The Outright Monetary Transactions program the speech foreshadowed was announced only in September and has never bought a bond. Yet two-year Italian and Spanish yields fell by roughly two percentage points around the OMT announcements \citep{AltavillaEtAl2016}, with the declines concentrated in default and redenomination premia rather than expected rates \citep{KrishnamurthyEtAl2018}. Three sentences repriced two of the largest bond markets in the world. The same three sentences, spoken in 2006 about the same two sovereigns, belonged to a different state of the boundary.

This paper is about when words and the stories they carry can move sovereign spreads, and it derives the answer from the default boundary. The narrative-economics program of \citet{Shiller2017} models stories as epidemics: beliefs that pass from person to person with a reproduction rate and a recovery rate. Applied to sovereign debt, the discipline is the default boundary. Stories about default are priced, and spread, where default is genuinely in play. The pricing half is a theorem from one primitive. The contagion half rests on one behavioral assumption and a numbered rival that the data separate.

The primitive is the shape of the default probability. A sovereign defaults when fundamentals cross a threshold, so its one-year default probability is an S-curve in current fundamentals: flat near zero for safe sovereigns, flat near one for condemned ones, steep in between. A narrative, in this model, is a portable pessimistic belief: investors who carry the story price the bond as if fundamentals were worse by a fixed amount $\xi$: the books are cooked, the union will break. The story's effect on the spread is then proportional to the probability the extra pessimism changes the default assessment, which is the S-curve's local slope: a hump that peaks where default hangs in the balance and vanishes in both tails. That is the pricing theorem. The contagion half follows from asking why anyone repeats a story. Following the fitness logic of \citet{FlynnSastry2024}, investors adopt a story when it is worth acting on, and a story about default is worth acting on exactly when it moves valuations: the adoption rate, and hence the epidemic reproduction number $R_0(\theta)$, inherits the same hump. Assumption~\ref{ass:adoption} states that adoption rule, and Section~\ref{sec:evidence} separates it from the rival rule in which adoption tracks perceived mispricing. One measurable object, the local sensitivity of spreads to fundamentals news, governs where stories are priced, where they spread, and, through the feedback from spreads to debt service, where they push the sovereign into default.

The theory delivers three regions. Where $R_0 < 1$, seeding a story produces a fizzle: prevalence decays geometrically and the pricing effect is the seed times the hump, a few basis points for safe sovereigns. Where $R_0 > 1$, a seed becomes an outbreak: prevalence converges to an endemic level and spreads carry a narrative premium for as long as fundamentals stay in the zone, viral, priced, and self-limiting. In an inner critical region, the loop closes: the outbreak raises spreads, spreads drain fundamentals through debt service, worse fundamentals raise both the story's price impact and its contagion rate, and the default basin shifts toward the sovereign. The geometry inverts the usual metaphor: the story moves the cliff. In the calibrated model, a sovereign at spread \SZoneBase\ basis points survives on the unseeded path and, seeded with a two percent prevalence, defaults at month \ZoneDefMonth: a slow narrative crisis, in the sense of \citet{LorenzoniWerning2019}, whose engine is epidemiology rather than equilibrium selection.

The quantitative claims are asymmetric. Narratives in this model move prices much more than they move defaults. At the hump's peak, ten percentage points of prevalence are worth \RelPeakTen\ basis points of spread, and a full outbreak generates an excess spread that reaches \PeakExcess\ basis points before the end: magnitudes in the range of the fundamentals-unexplained spread components documented for the euro periphery by \citet{DeGrauweJi2013}. The same outbreak adds up to \WedgePeak\ percentage points to the two-year default hazard at the baseline feedback, a band of \WedgeChiLo\ to \WedgeChiHi\ across the range of gross financing needs that disciplines the feedback parameter, all inside the zone. For a safe sovereign the addition is \WedgeSafe\ percentage points. A story taxes vulnerable sovereigns heavily while it circulates, and occasionally, in a narrow band of fundamentals, finishes one off.

The policy analysis divides interventions by what they do to the epidemic, and the division is sharp enough to be the paper's practical contribution. Counter-speech that deletes carriers, takedowns and official denials, buys time against a supercritical story: prevalence regrows at rate $\gamma(R_0 - 1)$, so removing three quarters of carriers postpones the outbreak by $\ln 4 / [\gamma(R_0 - 1)]$, about six weeks at the calibration's zone center. Transparency, modeled as a faster abandonment rate for stories contradicted by verifiable data, is genuine prophylaxis: it shrinks the susceptible zone from both ends and cuts the hazard wedge from \WedgePeak\ to \WedgeTransp\ percentage points. A credible spread cap, the whatever-it-takes instrument, breaks the link from prevalence to price, removes the story's decision-relevance, and starves the contagion itself: the wedge falls to \WedgeCap. The cap makes the story pointless. Its cost is time-sensitive, however, because the feedback loop runs while policy deliberates: at the calibration, each year of delay in introducing the cap costs \DelayCost\ percentage points of default probability, \DelayChiLo\ to \DelayChiHi\ across the financing-need band. July 2012 arrived roughly thirty months after Greek spreads first signaled the zone. The model prices that interval.

The paper links three literatures. From narrative economics it takes the epidemiological structure \citep{Shiller2017, KermackMcKendrick1927} and the fitness microfoundation \citep{FlynnSastry2024}, and gives the program what survey evidence says it needs, a setting where narratives are decision-relevant \citep{AndreEtAl2026}: here they are priced, and their prevalence and reproduction rate are estimable from text. From sovereign-debt theory it takes the default boundary and the service feedback \citep{EatonGersovitz1981, Arellano2008, Calvo1988, LorenzoniWerning2019}, and offers a mechanism for belief-driven crises in a unique period-by-period equilibrium. The belief object is a measurable epidemic rather than an unobservable selection device: closer in spirit to the sentiments of \citet{AngeletosLaO2013}, but with dynamics and a data counterpart. From the text-as-data literature \citep{GentzkowKellyTaddy2019, BakerBloomDavis2016, BybeeEtAl2020} it takes measurement tools, and returns a theory of which text objects should price: prevalence and reproduction of specific default narratives, with the boundary interaction supplying the identification.

The empirical strategy follows the theory's discipline. The behavioral core, that investors adopt stories in proportion to their observed price relevance, is tested against the rival rule in which adoption tracks perceived mispricing. The two rules separate under a credible cap. Causality is pursued with scheduled-release timing, cross-border story spillovers, and the theory's built-in placebo, outbreaks in immune countries.

Section~\ref{sec:policy} analyzes deletion, transparency, and the cap. Section~\ref{sec:quant} reports calibrated magnitudes. Section~\ref{sec:evidence} specifies measurement and empirical designs. Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes. Proofs and extensions are in the appendix.

\section{A Sovereign, Its Creditors, and a Story}\label{sec:model}

The analytical structure is simple. The pricing results, Lemma~\ref{lem:pricing} and Proposition~\ref{prop:hump}, are theorems from the threshold-default primitive alone. The contagion and policy results, Propositions~\ref{prop:zone} through \ref{prop:policy}, are theorems under Assumption~\ref{ass:adoption}. Section~\ref{sec:quant} attaches magnitudes to the fundamentals, payoff, and epidemic blocks, and Section~\ref{sec:evidence} maps the epidemic parameters to text moments.

\subsection{Fundamentals, default, and pricing}

Time is monthly, and pricing horizons are annual. A sovereign's fundamentals $\theta_t$ measure its debt-service capacity: the distance, in units of annual resources, between what the government can mobilize and what its obligations require. Fundamentals mean-revert and are drained by borrowing costs:
\begin{equation}
\theta_{t+1} = \rho_m \theta_t + (1 - \rho_m)\bar\theta - \chi_m s_t + \varepsilon_{t+1},
\qquad \varepsilon \sim N(0, \sigma_m^2),
\label{eq:theta-law}
\end{equation}
where $s_t$ is the spread on the sovereign's debt and $\chi_m$ converts a year of elevated refinancing costs into lost capacity: the debt-service feedback of \citet{Calvo1988} and \citet{LorenzoniWerning2019}. Default occurs when capacity is exhausted, $\theta_t < 0$. At annual horizon, with annualized parameters $(\rho, \sigma, \chi)$, the default probability perceived by an investor who believes fundamentals are $\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
p(\theta, s) = \Phi\!\left( z(\theta, s) \right),
\qquad
z(\theta, s) = \frac{-\rho\theta - (1-\rho)\bar\theta + \chi s}{\sigma}.
\label{eq:pdef}
\end{equation}
The S-shape of $p$ in $\theta$ is the paper's load-bearing object, and it comes free: any default technology in which a shock-buffeted state crosses a threshold produces one.

A pessimistic narrative circulates among the sovereign's creditors. Carrying the story means pricing the bond under fundamentals worse by $\xi$: the deficit is understated, the peg will break. The story is portable and self-contained, which is what lets it spread. Its content is a belief distortion rather than an information signal. With prevalence $n_t$, the fraction of investor wealth carrying the story, the market-clearing default assessment is the wealth-weighted average
\begin{equation}
\bar p(\theta, s, n) = (1 - n)\, \Phi(z) + n\, \Phi(z + \Delta),
\qquad \Delta \equiv \frac{\rho \xi}{\sigma},
\label{eq:pbar}
\end{equation}
so the story enters as a shift of size $\Delta$ in the standardized distance to default for the infected. Risk-neutral pricing of one-period debt with recovery $\mathcal{R}$ gives the spread as expected loss, $s = (1 - \mathcal{R})\bar p$, and since the spread feeds back into the default assessment through \eqref{eq:pdef}, the within-period equilibrium is a fixed point.

\begin{lemma}[Pricing equilibrium and the doom-loop multiplier]\label{lem:pricing}
If $(1-\mathcal{R})\chi / (\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}) < 1$, the map $s \mapsto (1-\mathcal{R})\bar p(\theta, s, n)$ is a contraction: for every $(\theta, n)$ there is a unique equilibrium spread $s^*(\theta, n)$, increasing in $n$ and decreasing in $\theta$. Small shocks to the default assessment are amplified by the multiplier
\[
M(\theta, n) = \Big[ 1 - (1-\mathcal{R})\,\tfrac{\chi}{\sigma}\big((1-n)\phi(z^*) + n\,\phi(z^* + \Delta)\big) \Big]^{-1} \,\ge\, 1,
\]
which peaks at the boundary and equals \MultPeak\ at its calibrated maximum. If the feedback crosses the contraction frontier, an interval of fundamentals admits multiple pricing equilibria: the \citet{Calvo1988} regime, mapped in Appendix~\ref{app:pricing}.
\end{lemma}

The calibration sits inside the uniqueness region with a maximal loop gain of \GainMax. The narrative moves a well-defined price in a unique equilibrium, which makes its prevalence a regressor rather than a sunspot.

\subsection{The contagion block}

Prevalence evolves by the logic of \citet{KermackMcKendrick1927}: carriers meet susceptibles and transmit, and carriers abandon the story at rate $\gamma$:
\begin{equation}
n_{t+1} - n_t = \beta(\theta_t, n_t)\, n_t (1 - n_t) - \gamma\, n_t.
\label{eq:sir}
\end{equation}
The economics is in $\beta$, and here the paper departs from specification toward microfoundation. A story is transmitted when a susceptible investor who hears it decides it is worth adopting, and an investor whose business is prices adopts a story in proportion to what it does to prices.

\begin{assumption}[Decision-relevant adoption]\label{ass:adoption}
Upon meeting a carrier, a susceptible investor adopts the story with probability $h(v)$, where $v = \partial s^* / \partial n$ is the story's observed price relevance, $h$ is increasing, and $h(0) = 0$: zero price relevance gives zero adoption. Meetings occur at rate $\bar m A$, where $A$ indexes the amplification environment: the reach and velocity of the media that carry financial stories. Hence $\beta(\theta, n) = \bar m A\, h\big(v(\theta, n)\big)$.
\end{assumption}

The assumption is the fitness principle of \citet{FlynnSastry2024} specialized to traders: narratives survive when they organize action, and in a bond market, action means repricing. Its main rival is adoption by \emph{perceived mispricing}, the gap between the market price and the infected valuation. The two rules agree everywhere except under a price peg, where relevance dies and mispricing survives, and Section~\ref{sec:policy} turns that disagreement into a test. Under Assumption~\ref{ass:adoption}, the story's epidemiology is pinned to its pricing: the reproduction number
\begin{equation}
R_0(\theta) = \frac{\beta(\theta, 0)}{\gamma} = \frac{\bar m A\, h\big(v(\theta, 0)\big)}{\gamma}
\label{eq:r0}
\end{equation}
is large exactly where the story is worth money, and the question of where stories can go viral becomes the question of where beliefs are priced. That question has an exact answer, and it is the next section's business.

\section{The Boundary Theorems}\label{sec:boundary}

\subsection{Where stories are priced}

\begin{proposition}[The pricing hump follows from the boundary]\label{prop:hump}
The story's equilibrium price impact is
\[
\frac{\partial s^*}{\partial n} = (1 - \mathcal{R})\,\big[\Phi(z^* + \Delta) - \Phi(z^*)\big]\, M(\theta, n).
\]
(i) The belief gap $\Phi(z^* + \Delta) - \Phi(z^*)$ is the mass of the fundamentals density inside a window of width $\Delta$: it is log-concave and single-peaked in $z^*$, maximal where the marginal default assessment is even, $\bar p \approx 1/2$, and vanishing in both tails. (ii) Since $z^*(\theta)$ is strictly decreasing, the primitive price relevance is single-peaked in fundamentals, converging to zero both for safe sovereigns ($\theta \to \infty$) and for condemned ones ($z^* \to \infty$). (iii) The multiplier $M \in [1, \bar M]$ is generated by the same normal density. In the calibrated range $\Delta \le 2$, it preserves the single hump of the equilibrium impact and distorts its level only within the bounded factor $\bar M$.
\end{proposition}

The proposition converts the pricing discipline into a theorem: the story shifts perceived fundamentals by a fixed amount, and the hump follows from the S-curve alone. Safe sovereigns absorb the story with only \RelSafeTen\ basis points per ten percentage points of prevalence. Condemned sovereigns already price default. The action is in between, where ten points of prevalence are worth up to \RelPeakTen\ basis points, and where the doom-loop multiplier also peaks.

\subsection{Where stories spread}

Under Assumption~\ref{ass:adoption}, the adoption probability is an increasing function of the price impact, so the epidemiology is a monotone transform of Proposition~\ref{prop:hump}.

\begin{proposition}[The susceptible zone]\label{prop:zone}
(i) $R_0(\theta)$ is single-peaked in $\theta$ with vanishing tails. (ii) The susceptible zone $Z = \{\theta : R_0(\theta) \ge 1\}$ is therefore an interval $[\theta_L, \theta_H]$, nonempty if and only if amplification clears a threshold, $\bar m A\, h(v_{\max}) \ge \gamma$. (iii) The zone widens with amplification $A$, with the story's bite $\xi$, with loss given default $1 - \mathcal{R}$, and with the feedback $\chi$. It shrinks with the abandonment rate $\gamma$.
\end{proposition}

Two features of the zone carry the paper's macro-history content. First, its existence is an era-dependent fact: at the calibration, cutting amplification to a quarter of baseline, a crude stand-in for the pre-social-media information environment, shrinks the zone to a set of width \ZoneWidthPre\ in fundamentals units, effectively a point (Figure~\ref{fig:zone}). Supercritical \emph{investor-to-investor} contagion at business-relevant speed requires amplification, and the model makes the required threshold explicit. Second, the zone has a spread-space address: at baseline it maps to sovereigns trading between roughly \ZoneSpreadLo\ and \ZoneSpreadHi\ basis points (Figure~\ref{fig:hump}b), which is where the euro periphery lived from 2010 to 2012 and where narrative measurement should concentrate its power.

\begin{corollary}[One statistic]\label{cor:stat}
Price impact, contagion, and amplification are increasing transforms of a single local object, the belief-gap mass $\Phi(z^* + \Delta) - \Phi(z^*)$, itself proportional (for modest $\xi$) to the density $\phi(z^*)$ that governs the sensitivity of spreads to fundamentals news. A country-time measure of that sensitivity, the local slope of spreads in news, is therefore a sufficient statistic for boundary proximity: the model instructs empirical work to interact narrative measures with it, rather than with debt ratios or ratings directly.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Three regions, and how a story becomes a crisis}

Combining the blocks, the state $(\theta_t, n_t)$ moves through a plane whose geometry the propositions have already drawn. Outside the zone, seeds decay at rate $\gamma(1 - R_0) > 0$, and the cumulative spread effect is bounded by the seed times the hump. Inside the zone, seeds grow logistically toward the endemic level $n^\infty = 1 - \gamma/\beta$, and spreads carry a narrative premium for as long as fundamentals remain febrile. Whether the premium becomes a crisis depends on the feedback.

\begin{proposition}[The moving cliff]\label{prop:cliff}
Let $D(\theta, n) = (1-\rho_m)(\bar\theta - \theta) - \chi_m s^*(\theta, n)$ be the deterministic drift of fundamentals. (i) The baseline basin boundary $\hat\theta(0)$, below which drift is negative along the deterministic path, rises with prevalence: $\hat\theta(n)$ is increasing, because $s^*$ is increasing in $n$. (ii) There is therefore a critical interval $(\hat\theta(0), \hat\theta(n^\infty))$ of initial fundamentals at which the sovereign survives along the unseeded path and defaults along the seeded path, whenever the zone and the interval intersect. The intersection is nonempty at the calibration. (iii) Within the critical interval the crisis is slow: the outbreak must first build prevalence and then drain fundamentals, so default arrives with a lag governed by $\gamma(R_0 - 1)$ and $\chi_m$ rather than by a jump.
\end{proposition}

Part (i) is the geometry worth remembering: prevalence moves the cliff toward the sovereign. Part (iii) connects the mechanism to the crisis phenomenology it is meant to explain. Narrative crises in this model are months in which prevalence and spreads rise while fundamentals erode, visible in text data the whole way down: the slow-moving crises of \citet{LorenzoniWerning2019}, with an epidemic where their perfect foresight was. In the calibrated exhibit of Section~\ref{sec:quant}, a sovereign seeded at two percent prevalence crosses the boundary at month \ZoneDefMonth. Its unseeded twin, facing identical fundamentals, survives indefinitely.

\section{Policy: Deletion, Transparency, and the Cap}\label{sec:policy}

An intervention against a narrative can remove carriers, speed abandonment, or destroy the story's relevance. The three have different mathematics, and the differences are the policy content of the paper.

\begin{proposition}[The counter-narrative trilemma]\label{prop:policy}
(i) \emph{Deletion is postponement.} A one-shot removal of a fraction $f$ of carriers, with $R_0(\theta) > 1$ along the path, delays the endemic outbreak by approximately $\ln\!\big(\tfrac{1}{1-f}\big) / \big[\gamma(R_0 - 1)\big]$: at the calibrated zone center, deleting three quarters of carriers buys $1.5$ months. (ii) \emph{Transparency is prophylaxis.} Raising the abandonment rate $\gamma$, by making the story cheaper to check against verifiable data, scales $R_0$ down everywhere: it shrinks the zone from both ends, lowers the endemic level, and cuts the crisis wedge. (iii) \emph{A credible cap is sterilization.} A facility that pegs the spread at $\bar s$ whenever the market would exceed it sets $\partial s^*/\partial n = 0$ wherever it binds. Under Assumption~\ref{ass:adoption} adoption stops, $R_0$ falls below one, and the epidemic dies. The cap also severs the feedback $\chi s$, so it removes the critical region even for stories already endemic.
\end{proposition}

Part (i) is the postponement formula. Against a supercritical story, removing carriers buys the logarithmic delay above and leaves the growth rate intact. Part (iii) is the model's reading of July 2012. The speech, and the OMT design behind it, functioned as a conditional peg on the object the redenomination story needed to move. Once price relevance disappeared, the story stopped being worth telling, and both prevalence and price collapsed. The required credibility is computable. If markets attach probability $c$ to activation, the story's price relevance in capped states is $1 - c$ times its uncapped value, and with adoption proportional to relevance the reproduction number scales the same way: sterilization requires $c \ge 1 - 1/R_0(\theta)$, which at the calibrated zone peak of $R_0 = \RZeroPeak$ means the facility must be believed with probability three quarters. A weaker peg leaves a residual wedge equal to the uncapped wedge scaled by surviving relevance. The instrument is time-sensitive because the feedback runs while institutions deliberate: the quantitative section prices delay at \DelayCost\ percentage points of two-year default probability per year.

The disagreement between adoption rules introduced at Assumption~\ref{ass:adoption} becomes observable here. If adoption tracks price relevance, a credible cap collapses narrative \emph{volume}: text measures should fall after the announcement. If adoption tracks perceived mispricing, the story survives unpriced: volume persists while spreads sit at the cap. The distinction is measurable in post-OMT text, and Section~\ref{sec:evidence} registers it as a prediction.

\section{Quantitative Magnitudes}\label{sec:quant}

\subsection{Calibration}

Table~\ref{tab:calib7} lists parameters. The fundamentals block sets annual persistence \RhoVal\ and innovation \SigVal, so the unconditional standard deviation of capacity is about $0.11$ against a long-run level of $0.15$: default, at the boundary $\theta = 0$, is a genuine tail event for a healthy sovereign and a live one inside the zone. Recovery of $0.60$ matches mean haircuts near 40 percent \citep{CrucesTrebesch2013}. The feedback $\chi$ is an accounting identity. \section{Measurement and Empirical Designs}\label{sec:evidence}

\subsection{Measuring an epidemic in text}

The model's belief object is a specific, portable story with a prevalence and a reproduction rate, and both are estimable. The measurement protocol, specified fully in Appendix~\ref{app:data}, has four layers. First, the story universe: five sovereign-debt narrative categories, default and restructuring, bailout and IMF rescue, redenomination and exit, fiscal-sustainability collapse, and the bank-sovereign loop, defined by claim structure rather than keyword lists. Second, classification: country-day text from newswires, national press, and platform data where licensed, classified by a transparent dictionary layer plus a supervised language-model layer whose prompts, labels, and validation statistics are all reported. \citet{GentzkowKellyTaddy2019} supply the standards, and the policy-uncertainty index of \citet{BakerBloomDavis2016} the precedent for index construction from counts. Third, the epidemiological quantities: prevalence $\hat n_{i,t}$ as the reach-weighted share of country-$i$ financial coverage carrying the story, and reproduction $\hat R_{i,t}$ from the cascade structure, the rate at which the story jumps outlets and platforms, in the spirit of the news-structure decompositions of \citet{BybeeEtAl2020}. Fourth, validation: hand-labeled samples with inter-rater statistics, and the requirement, from \citet{AndreEtAl2026}, that text-based prevalence predict survey beliefs as well as prices.

The protocol maps text coverage into the model's wealth-weighted prevalence. The raw text object is reach-weighted coverage. The validation layer links that object to investor beliefs: reach-weighted coverage must predict surveyed crash beliefs, and the survey pass-through pins down the scale factor from coverage to the wealth share $n$ that enters the pricing equation.

\subsection{Four designs}

\noindent\textbf{Design 1: the boundary interaction.} The workhorse is a state-dependent local projection,
\begin{equation}
\Delta s_{i,t+h} = \alpha_i + \tau_t + \beta_h\, \hat n_{i,t} + \delta_h\, \hat n_{i,t} \times B_{i,t-1} + \Gamma_h' X_{i,t-1} + u_{i,t+h},
\label{eq:lp7}
\end{equation}
with $B_{i,t}$ the sufficient statistic of Corollary~\ref{cor:stat}: the rolling sensitivity of country $i$'s spread to common fundamentals news, estimated from scheduled-release windows rather than a debt ratio or rating. The model predicts $\beta_h \approx 0$ and $\delta_h > 0$: prevalence prices where the hump is high, with the profile of $\delta_h$ across $B$ bins tracing Figure~\ref{fig:hump}a. Global factors in the sense of \citet{LongstaffEtAl2011} enter $X$ and $\tau_t$. State-dependence is the theory's signature: reverse causality running uniformly from spreads to coverage produces a level relation, while the model predicts a hump.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Design 2: scheduled releases, surprise against take-up.} Scheduled fiscal releases separate information from its narrative echo: the surprise is the released number against consensus, and the take-up is the narrative prevalence response in the following 48 hours, orthogonalized on the surprise. The model predicts the take-up premium, the spread response to take-up beyond the surprise's direct effect, is concentrated inside the zone, and that identical surprises generate larger take-up where $R_0$ is higher: contagion does the marginal work.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Design 3: imported stories.} Stories cross borders faster than fundamentals do. A crisis in country $j$ raises the global prevalence of the default story, and its arrival in country $i$'s discourse is predicted by pre-determined media, language, and investor-overlap exposure. Exposure-weighted foreign story shocks therefore instrument domestic prevalence, with real linkages, trade, bank claims, and common creditors controlled directly. The model's sharp prediction is about \emph{where} imported stories price: the same hump. Platform outages and algorithm changes supply auxiliary transmission shocks.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Design 4: structural estimation and surveillance.} Simulated method of moments on $(\xi, \chi, A, \gamma)$, with moments chosen for state-dependence: the LP coefficients by boundary bin, outbreak persistence and final-size statistics, the text-spread lead-lag profile, and one event moment, the speed of prevalence collapse after the OMT announcements, which doubles as the test separating relevance-based from mispricing-based adoption (Proposition~\ref{prop:policy}). The estimated model then runs forward as surveillance: country-time estimates $\hat R_{0,i,t}$, an epidemiological early-warning object with a structural interpretation, validated against the 2010--2012 chronology held outside estimation.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

The narrative hypothesis in macroeconomics has suffered from explaining too much. This paper's contribution is a boundary: a model in which stories about sovereign default are priced and spread, and occasionally push the sovereign into crisis, inside a derived interval of fundamentals, and in which every step of that sentence is measurable. One primitive, the S-curve of default probability, generates the pricing hump, the contagion zone, and the doom-loop amplification as three faces of the same local statistic: where beliefs are priced, stories about beliefs are worth telling. The equilibrium is unique throughout, and the belief object is an epidemic with an estimable prevalence and reproduction number rather than a residual with a name.

The quantitative lessons are asymmetric. Stories tax vulnerable sovereigns heavily, up to \RelPeakTen\ basis points per ten points of prevalence, thousands at outbreak peak, and change outcomes rarely: up to \WedgePeak\ percentage points of two-year default hazard, in a narrow band. The policy lessons rank interventions by their epidemiology: deletion postpones, transparency inoculates, and a credible cap sterilizes, at a price of \DelayCost\ percentage points per year of delay. The whatever-it-takes speech, on this reading, destroyed a story's reason to exist.

The empirical half delivers the classified story archive, the prevalence and reproduction series, and the estimated $(\xi, \chi, A, \gamma)$ that convert the calibrated magnitudes into measurements. The payoff extends beyond this paper: an epidemiological surveillance system for sovereign debt markets, tracking which stories are spreading, how fast, and whether the sovereign beneath them is standing in the crisis zone.

\section{Proofs}\label{app:proofs}

\subsection{Proof of Lemma \ref{lem:pricing}}
Write $F(s) = (1-\mathcal{R})\bar p(\theta, s, n)$. Then $F'(s) = (1-\mathcal{R})\tfrac{\chi}{\sigma}\big[(1-n)\phi(z) + n\phi(z+\Delta)\big] \le (1-\mathcal{R})\tfrac{\chi}{\sigma}\phi(0) < 1$ under the stated condition, so $F$ maps $[0, 1-\mathcal{R}]$ into itself with slope uniformly below one: a contraction, with unique fixed point $s^*$. Monotonicity in $(\theta, n)$ follows from $\bar p$ increasing in $n$ (since $\Phi(z+\Delta) > \Phi(z)$) and decreasing in $\theta$, plus the contraction property. The multiplier is $1/(1 - F'(s^*))$ by the implicit function theorem. If instead $(1-\mathcal{R})\chi\phi(0)/\sigma > 1$, $F$ is S-shaped with slope exceeding one near $z^* = 0$, and an interval of $\theta$ admits three fixed points: Appendix~\ref{app:pricing}. A cap replaces $F$ by $\min(F, \bar s)$, preserving the contraction. $\blacksquare$

\subsection{Proof of Proposition \ref{prop:hump}}
Let $G(z)=\Phi(z+\Delta)-\Phi(z)=\int_z^{z+\Delta}\phi(u)\,du$. Since $\phi'(u)=-u\phi(u)$,
\[
G'(z)=\phi(z+\Delta)-\phi(z).
\]
For $\Delta>0$, equality $\phi(z+\Delta)=\phi(z)$ holds iff $(z+\Delta)^2=z^2$, hence iff $z=-\Delta/2$. If $z<-\Delta/2$, then $|z+\Delta|<|z|$ and $G'(z)>0$. If $z>-\Delta/2$, then $|z+\Delta|>|z|$ and $G'(z)<0$. Thus $G$ is single-peaked at $-\Delta/2$ and converges to zero in both tails. Differentiating the pricing fixed point gives
\[
\frac{dz^*}{d\theta}=-\frac{\rho}{\sigma}\,M(\theta,n)<0,
\]
so the single-peaked profile in $z$ maps into a single-peaked profile in fundamentals. For the equilibrium impact, $1\le M\le \bar M=[1-(1-\mathcal{R})\chi\phi(0)/\sigma]^{-1}$, and $M\to 1$ in both tails because the normal density vanishes there. Hence $(1-\mathcal{R})G(z^*)M$ vanishes in both tails and stays within the bounded factor $\bar M$ of the primitive hump. The calibrated narrative shift has $\Delta=\ShiftVal$ and the sensitivity grid stays below two. On that region, $G$ and $M$ rise together on the left of the hump and fall together on the right, with the bridge contained in the concave part of the normal density. The product therefore has one peak at the boundary region shown in Figure~\ref{fig:hump}. $\blacksquare$

\subsection{Proof of Proposition \ref{prop:zone}}
$R_0 = \bar m A\, h(v)/\gamma$ with $h$ increasing and $v$ single-peaked with vanishing tails (Proposition~\ref{prop:hump}): $R_0$ is single-peaked with vanishing tails, so its superlevel set at one is an interval, nonempty iff the peak clears one, which is the stated amplification threshold. Comparative statics: $A$ scales $R_0$, $\xi$ widens the window defining $G$, $1-\mathcal{R}$ and $\chi$ scale $v$ through the pricing equation and the multiplier, and $\gamma$ divides. Each moves $R_0$ monotonically at every $\theta$, so the superlevel interval moves as stated. $\blacksquare$

\subsection{Proof of Proposition \ref{prop:cliff}}
(i) $D(\theta, n)$ is strictly decreasing in $n$ because $s^*$ is strictly increasing in $n$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:pricing}) wherever $G > 0$. Define $\hat\theta(n) = \sup\{\theta : D(\theta, n) < 0\}$: since the set $\{D < 0\}$ expands with $n$, $\hat\theta$ is nondecreasing, strictly where the pricing hump is active. (ii) For $\theta_0 > \hat\theta(0)$ the unseeded deterministic path has $D > 0$ throughout and converges upward toward $\bar\theta$: survival. For seeded paths with $\theta_0$ in the zone, prevalence grows toward $n^\infty$, and once $n_t$ is large enough that $\hat\theta(n_t) > \theta_t$, drift turns negative and both $\theta_t$ and, through the hump, $R_0$ move adversely: the pair enters a trapping region and crosses the boundary in finite time. The set of $\theta_0$ for which this occurs is nonempty whenever $(\hat\theta(0), \hat\theta(n^\infty))$ meets the zone. At the calibration $\theta_0 = 0.06$ exhibits it. (iii) The default time decomposes into the outbreak's build time, of order $\ln(n^\dagger/n_0)/[\gamma(R_0-1)]$ for the prevalence $n^\dagger$ at which drift turns negative, plus the drain time $(\theta_0 - 0)/|\bar D|$: both are months-to-years for calibrated parameters, never a jump. $\blacksquare$

\subsection{Proof of Proposition \ref{prop:policy}}
(i) With $R_0 > 1$ roughly constant along the path, linearized prevalence grows as $n_{t} \approx n_{0}e^{gt}$, $g = \gamma(R_0 - 1)$, and a cut to $(1-f)n$ is undone when $e^{gt} = 1/(1-f)$. The logistic's later stages and final size depend only on $\beta, \gamma$: unchanged. (ii) $\gamma$ enters $R_0$ as a divisor: the superlevel set of the single-peaked $R_0$ shrinks from both ends, and $n^\infty = 1 - \gamma/\beta$ falls at every interior $\theta$. (iii) Where the cap binds, $s^*$ is locally constant in $n$, so $v = 0$, $h(0) = 0$, $\beta = 0 < \gamma$: prevalence decays at rate $\gamma$ regardless of $R_0$'s uncapped value. A cap at or below the zone's entry spread binds throughout the zone. The calibrated 400 bp cap leaves a sliver of the zone's upper edge unbound where $R_0$ barely exceeds one, and the measured crisis wedge there is zero (Figure~\ref{fig:crisis}b). Severing $\chi s$ above the cap also raises $D$, shrinking $\{D < 0\}$ and hence the critical region, for any prevalence path. $\blacksquare$

\section{The Pricing Block: Details and the Multiplicity Frontier}\label{app:pricing}

\paragraph{Spread mapping.} Linear expected-loss pricing $s = (1-\mathcal{R})\bar p$ understates convexity at high $\bar p$. The exact zero-profit mapping $s = -\log(1 - \bar p(1-\mathcal{R}))$ steepens the hump and widens the zone, so the text's linear choice is conservative. Endogenizing the promised yield through an issuance requirement adds a second loop with the same sign.

\paragraph{The Calvo frontier.} The uniqueness condition $(1-\mathcal{R})\chi\phi(0)/\sigma < 1$ defines a feedback frontier. At the calibration the maximal gain is \GainMax, and at the top of the GFN band ($\chi = \ChiGFNHi$) it reaches \GainChiHi: crisis-level financing needs sit at the frontier's edge while remaining below it. Past one, an interval of fundamentals carries three pricing equilibria, the static multiplicity of \citet{Calvo1988} studied quantitatively by \citet{AguiarEtAl2022}. Inside that region, a story can additionally coordinate jumps between equilibria. The present calibration stays in the unique region, where every narrative effect is attributable and measurable.

\paragraph{Wealth-weighted pricing.} Averaging beliefs by wealth presumes infected and susceptible investors hold with equal intensity. Letting the infected also shorten duration or exit produces an effective prevalence exceeding $n$ in the pricing equation and strengthens every result. The text uses the lower-intensity baseline.

\section{Simulation Details}\label{app:sim}

Monthly parameters derive from annual ones by $\rho_m = \rho^{1/12}$, $\sigma_m = \sigma/\sqrt{12}$, $\chi_m = \chi/12$. The pricing fixed point is solved by damped iteration to $10^{-12}$ on a $226 \times 51$ grid over $(\theta, n) \in [-0.10, 0.35] \times [0, 1]$, bilinearly interpolated in simulation. Monte Carlo experiments use 8{,}000 paths of 24 months with common random numbers across configurations, seed fixed. Default is absorbing at $\theta < 0$. Deterministic exhibits shut the fundamentals shock and freeze paths at the boundary. The one-shot deletion experiment removes 75 percent of prevalence in the stated month, and the cap experiment switches the pricing table from uncapped to capped from the stated month onward. All figures and every number in the text are generated by the accompanying Julia and R code from a single JSON of results.

\section{Data and Measurement Protocol}\label{app:data}

\paragraph{Sources.} Newswire and press text via Factiva/LexisNexis and GDELT, platform text where licensed, consensus forecasts for scheduled-release surprises, sovereign bond and CDS spreads at daily frequency, scheduled fiscal-release calendars, debt-management-office auction results, and media-reach weights from circulation and audience data.

\paragraph{Classification.} Each document is classified into the five narrative categories by (i) a transparent dictionary layer requiring a sovereign-solvency claim structure, subject, verb class, and horizon, beyond keywords alone, and (ii) a supervised language-model layer trained on hand-labeled samples, with prompts, label definitions, training splits, and error matrices published in full. Inter-rater reliability on the hand-labeled core and out-of-sample precision/recall by category are reported alongside every index release.

\paragraph{Construction.} Prevalence $\hat n_{i,t}$: reach-weighted share of country-$i$ sovereign-finance coverage carrying the category, smoothed at the weekly frequency the SIR block implies. Reproduction $\hat R_{i,t}$: cascade growth across outlets and platforms within story clusters, estimated by the standard epidemiological generation-interval method applied to first-appearance timestamps. Validation: correlation with survey-based crash beliefs where available \citep{AndreEtAl2026}, and the P2 placebo, prevalence spikes for safe sovereigns with near-zero price response.

\section{Extensions}\label{app:ext}

\paragraph{Cross-border story networks.} Let prevalence in country $i$ respond to a reach-weighted sum of foreign prevalences. Stories then comove across borders more than fundamentals do, generating correlated spread movements among zone countries that fundamentals-only models treat as common factors: a candidate microfoundation for the common factors of \citet{LongstaffEtAl2011}, and the structure behind Design 3.

\paragraph{Endogenous amplification.} Attention to sovereign stories rises with volatility, making $A$ procyclical: the zone widens exactly when fundamentals deteriorate, an accelerant with the same signature as a rising multiplier and a natural explanation for why crisis eras feel narrative-dense.

\paragraph{Immunity and waves.} Investors who abandoned a story once may resist it: an SIRS structure with slow waning of immunity produces refractory periods after outbreaks and recurring waves at business-cycle frequency, testable in long text archives: the same story striking the same country twice should spread slower the second time, controlling for the boundary.

\paragraph{Heterogeneous stories.} A mixture over bite sizes $\xi$ concentrates the priced composition toward severe stories as the boundary nears, since the hump's window widens with $\Delta$: near the boundary, discourse should grow and darken, a compositional prediction the classification layer can test.
